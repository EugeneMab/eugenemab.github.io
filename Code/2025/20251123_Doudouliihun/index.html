Introduction

Principles: convert Latin words into {Consonant-Soft-Vowel-Soft-Nasal} {C-S-V-S-N} structure

Step 1: simplify consonants and consonant clusters

Simplify consonants and consonant clusters to the consonants as below by the rules as below.

{b}{p}{m}{f}{v}
{d}{t}{n}{s}{j}{ts}{dz}
{g}{k}{h}{w}{l}

Rules:

  • {ph} => {f};
  • {w} => {v};
  • {th} => {t};
  • {c} ([ s ]), {ch} ([ ʃ ]/[ tʃ ]), {sh} ([ ʃ ]), {t} ([ ʃ ]/[ tʃ ]), {x} ([ s ]/[ ʃ ]) => {ts};
  • {j}, {z} => {dz};
  • {y} ([ j ]) => {j};
  • {c} ([ k ]), {ch} ([ k ]), {q} => {k};
  • {r} => {w};
  • {x} ([ ks ]) => {k} {s}

Step 2: convert vowels

Convert vowel {y} to {i}.

Step 3: Convert the consonants

Classify the syllable structure as below.

  • The vowel group is composed of first vowel (FV), middle vowel (MV), and last vowel (LV).
  • The first consonant before vowels is primary consonant (PC).
  • The first nasal after vowels is primary nasal (PN).
  • Other consonants are supporting consonants (SC), which includes before-vowel supporting consonants (BSC), and after-vowel supporting consonants (ASC).

  • PC BSC1 BSC2 FV MV LV ASC1 ASC2 ASC3 PN ASC4
  • SC = BSC1 BSC2 ASC1 ASC2 ASC3 ASC4

Organize using the rules. {#} means {number of}. {&} means {and}. {=>} means {if ... then ...}. {X() Y() Z()} are to follow the conversion table.

  • #PN = 1 & #SC = 0 => PC FV MV LV PN
  • #PN = 1 & #SC = 1 & #BSC = 1 => PC X(BSC1) FV MV LV PN
  • #PN = 1 & #SC = 1 & #ASC = 1 => PC FV MV LV Y(ASC1) PN
  • #PN = 1 & #SC >= 2 => PC X(SC1) FV MV LV Y(SC2) PN
  • #PN = 0 & #SC = 0 => PC FV MV LV
  • #PN = 0 & #SC = 1 & #BSC = 1 => PC X(BSC1) FV MV LV
  • #PN = 0 & #SC = 1 & #ASC = 1 => PC FV MV LV Y(ASC1)
  • #PN = 0 & #SC = 2 => PC X(SC1) FV MV LV Y(SC2)
  • #PN = 0 & #SC >= 3 => PC X(SC1) FV MV LV Y(SC2) Z(SC3)

  • Use modal verb suffixes, and make it optional to use natural ending-vowel
  • Change the score of {g} to 3 from 2
  • Add some better choices to main words

  • Font and color

  • Upper case and lower case
  • Add more examples
  • Use Unicode to show the phonetic symbols; Use UTF-8 encoding in Chinese version
  • Fix some wrong examples
  • Remove subjective sentences
  • Correct the phonetic annotation [a]=>[ɑ] [e]=>[ɛ] [o]=>[ɔ]
  • Add the translation of Doudouling
  • Add some discussion

2014 Language changes

  • Change {i-} to {g-}
  • Change {ch} [z] to {ch] [j]

2017 Language changes

  • IPA alignment: {h} [ŋ] > {h} [h]; {r} [h] > {w} [w]; {ph} [w] > {ts} [ts]; {z} [ts] > {j} [j]; {ch} [j] > {dz} [dz]
  • Merge {th} and {ch} into {dz} [dz]
  • Redesign of the ending and the stress
  • Long form and short form
  • Use short form when a single-syllable word is the center of phrase
  • Make all key words in {C-V} format

2018 Language changes

  • Use weak syllables to compensate, merge long form and short form

2019 Language changes

  • Insert compensating letter combinations before the last vowel
  • Stress at last vowel

2022 Language changes

  • Use double vowels to indicate the compensating sound
  • Keep all compensating sound near original location
  • Nasal marker

2025 Language changes

  • Use single vowel, or {iu}, or {iu} to indicate the compensating sound
  • Use {ii} or {uu} indicate the non-compensating vowels
  • Organize, truncate, and soften the compensating sound to form the Consonant-Soft-Vowel-Soft-Nasal structure
  • Remove nasal marker
